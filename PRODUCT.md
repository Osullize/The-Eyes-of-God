# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are the founder/operator and company senior leadership. They use the product as an internal business-development command console to discover overseas heat-pump sales leads, monitor search and crawling tasks, inspect collected company data, and prepare customer profiles for later AI-assisted scoring.

The context is operational rather than promotional: users need to make fast decisions from incomplete external website data, compare batches of companies, and trust that task status, database records, and AI profile outputs are traceable.

## Product Purpose

The product is a lead-generation and customer-intelligence backend for overseas heat-pump markets. It coordinates keyword groups, official-site discovery, website crawling, candidate groups, company libraries, raw database views, and AI profile analysis.

Success means the user can turn broad market keywords into a structured company pipeline: search results become candidate domains, candidate domains become crawled company evidence, and crawled evidence becomes higher-value customer profiles that can support sales prioritization.

## Brand Personality

Professional, calm, and aggressively capable.

The interface should feel technical and executive-grade: precise enough for operators, clear enough for leadership review, and forceful enough to communicate that this is a serious market-intelligence system rather than a lightweight scraping toy.

## Anti-references

- Do not look like a generic SaaS landing page.
- Do not use playful, toy-like, consumer-app styling.
- Do not bury workflows inside decorative cards or marketing copy.
- Do not use visual effects that distract from task execution, table scanning, or evidence review.
- Do not make the system feel like a one-off script wrapper; it should feel like a durable internal platform.

## Design Principles

1. Make the workflow legible: every major screen should reveal where the user is in the lead pipeline and what action is available next.
2. Preserve operator trust: task status, raw records, candidate groups, and AI outputs should feel traceable and auditable.
3. Prioritize dense clarity: show enough data for comparison without overwhelming the user with ornament.
4. Use technical confidence sparingly: technical atmosphere should come from structure, hierarchy, and precise controls, not decorative noise.
5. Serve both execution and review: the same interface must support hands-on task running and leadership-level inspection.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Support reduced-motion preferences. Avoid meaning conveyed by color alone, especially for task status, lead priority, and error states. Keep Chinese UI labels readable at dashboard density and avoid layouts that depend on long English-only text widths.
